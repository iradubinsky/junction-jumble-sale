Giant Junction Jumble Sale 2026

Participant Guide

Everything you need to have a great sale day

What to Sell

👕 Clothing & Accessories

Best sellers are gently used name brands, vintage items, and children's clothing. Avoid heavily worn or stained pieces unless priced very low.

🛋️ Furniture & Home Goods

Small furniture is gold: side tables, chairs, lamps, mirrors. Kitchen appliances and cookware are consistent top performers.

🎮 Electronics & Media

Working electronics sell fast. DVDs, CDs, and video games are back in fashion. Always mark electronics as "tested/working" — consider letting buyers plug in and test on the spot.

🧸 Toys, Games & Kids Items

Complete board games, LEGO (by the bag works great), bikes, and baby gear are high-demand. Incomplete games should be marked down or sold as-is.

🔧 Tools & Sporting Goods

Hand tools, garden tools, camping gear, and exercise equipment draw serious buyers who will often pay fair prices.

📚 Books

Price low and sell in bundles. A "3 for $1" or "$5 bag" strategy clears inventory fast. Children's books and cookbooks tend to sell best individually.

Recommended Pricing

The golden rule: price at 10–30% of original retail value.

Display & Setup Tips

Use tables wherever possibleItems on the ground get ignored. Borrow folding tables, use plywood on sawhorses, or stack boxes under tablecloths. Canadian Tire sells a great folding table for ~$60 that stores easily and you'll use again.
Group like with likeClothing together, kitchen items together, books in a bin. Shoppers think categorically.
Hang clothing when possibleA simple rack or clothesline dramatically increases clothing sales compared to piles on a table.
Face items toward the streetPut your best, most eye-catching pieces at the curb to catch drive-by attention.
Label everythingUnpriced items get put back down. Shoppers hate asking. Consider colour-coded stickers: red = $1, blue = $2 — it lets you reduce prices dynamically as the day winds down.

Pricing Psychology

Bundle slow movers"5 books for $2" or "Fill a bag for $5" clears volume fast.
Use odd pricing$1.50 feels more deliberate than $2 and buyers are less likely to negotiate.
Plan your markdown scheduleStart full price in the morning, discount 25–50% after noon, and go "make an offer" in the final hour.
Don't be offended by hagglersNegotiation is expected. If you're not willing to budge, price 20% higher than your minimum.

Day-Of Logistics

Start setup 1 hour before openingEarly birds will show up before the official 8:30AM start time.
Bring plenty of changeAt least $50 in small bills and coins. Most transactions will be cash.
Offer bagsA great way to get rid of all those reusable shopping bags you've been hoarding.
Use a cash box or fanny packKeep money secure and on your person at all times.
Keep an extension cord handySo buyers can test electronics on the spot.
Have cleaning supplies nearbyA quick wipe-down of a dusty item before handing it over goes a long way.
Put out balloons on the dayA great way to draw in passersby who aren't specifically looking for the sale.

Before & After

Spread the wordShare the Facebook event and tell your friends and neighbours. The more traffic we collectively drive, the better time everyone will have.
Add your home to the mapRegister at giantjunctionjumblesale.ca so shoppers can find you.
Food & drinks welcomeFresh baked goods, water, and popsicles will be a welcome addition and a great way to get kids engaged in the sale.
Plan for unsold itemsDecide in advance: donate to Goodwill or Salvation Army, list on Facebook Marketplace, or schedule a charity pickup. Don't let it all drift back into your house.
